Column: Why Distributed Network Architecture?
This column explains the structural necessity of moving from centralized packet-switching to a Structure-Native distributed architecture.

The Core Problem: Centralized Limits
The Manhole Bottleneck: Physical congestion in central offices and urban manholes is reaching its limit.

The AI Gap: Adding AI as a software overlay cannot solve the physical latency and single points of failure inherent in centralized designs.

The Velsanet Solution: Structure Switching
Topology-First: Defines network space through 5 Platonic geometries (T₄, C₆, O₈, D₁₂, I₂₀) before addressing.

Deterministic Performance: Physically isolated optical paths eliminate packet collisions and queuing delays.

Embedded Intelligence: A three-layer AI (PAI, AAI, AsAI) is built into the network structure itself.

Vision
Velsanet is a new global network foundation designed for absolute reliability and decentralized governance, ensuring the network belongs to all of humanity.
